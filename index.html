The Six Families — how every spice is organised
Builders
Go in first.
Need fat and heat.
The invisible foundation of every complex dish.
Warmers
Sweet-savoury depth.
Early to mid.
Cinnamon, clove, ginger, nutmeg.
Aromatics
Fragrance-led.
Never accidental.
Cardamom, saffron, star anise.
Savoury Herbs
Earthy depth.
Hold through cooking.
Oregano, thyme, rosemary, bay.
Heat Bringers
Heat and sharpness.
Flexible timing.
Pepper, chili, cayenne, mustard.
Brighteners
Lift and acidity.
Always last.
Heat destroys them. Sumac, mint, dill.

Three things to remember
Start with Builders
Every great dish starts with a Builder base — garlic, onion, cumin, coriander. These go in first with fat and heat. Without them, everything feels thin.
One lead spice per dish
Never stack two very high impact spices. Clove (4), cayenne (5), saffron (4) — each wants to lead. Pick one and let everything else support it.
Brighteners always go last
Sumac, mint, dill, amchur — these are the finishing touches. Add them off heat or right at the end. Heat destroys the volatile acids that make them work.
